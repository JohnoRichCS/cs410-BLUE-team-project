AI-Assisted Fact Checking Tool to Combat Online Misinformation

A cross-platform concept for helping users quickly verify online claims by combining AI analysis, source checking, and evidence-backed credibility assessments.

View Presentations

Project Overview

Elevator Pitch

Online misinformation spreads rapidly across social media, news sites, and online communities, making it difficult for users to decipher what information they can trust, especially with the rise of AI-generated content. Existing fact-checking organizations can be limited due to recent AI innovations and usually can only verify highly viral content or posts on specific platforms.

This project proposes an AI-assisted fact-checking tool that allows users to verify information quickly by analyzing text, articles, or social media posts across the internet. The system will evaluate claims, search reliable sources, and provide a credibility assessment with supporting evidence.

The goal is to help users identify misleading information faster and make more informed decisions based on the content they consume.

Problem Description

Internet users are constantly subjected to rising levels of misinformation stemming from online sources and platforms. The problem is made worse by AI-generated posts, articles, and social media accounts, while current fact-checking features are often limited to specific platforms and levels of post engagement. Users still lack a universal, accessible, on-demand method for quickly verifying the information they encounter online.

Customer and End User

The primary end users are internet users who regularly consume content through social media, websites, and online communities and want a quick, easy-to-use way to verify claims. This can include students, everyday readers, researchers, and professionals who need better tools for checking credibility online.

Solution Statement

The proposed solution is an AI-assisted fact-checking tool that can take links, copied text, and social media content as input, analyze the claims, compare them against trusted sources, and return a credibility assessment with supporting evidence. The tool is intended to be accessible through a browser extension or mobile application.

Scope

What It Will Do / Will Not Do

What It Will Do

  • Accept links to articles, social media posts, and pasted text.
  • Use AI models and web searches to analyze claims within content.
  • Compare claims against trusted sources and databases.
  • Provide a credibility assessment such as likely true, misleading, false, or uncertain.
  • Display supporting evidence and references used in the evaluation.
  • Support quick fact checking through a browser extension or mobile app.

What It Will Not Do

  • Guarantee 100% accuracy in credibility assessments.
  • Automatically remove or censor online content.
  • Track users or store personal browsing data long-term.
  • Provide political or ideological opinions.
  • Replace human or user fact-checking entirely.

Monetization

Potential Monetization Strategies

Premium Tier

Could include faster analysis, deeper claim breakdowns, and expanded source databases.

Enterprise / API Licensing

Could offer an API for organizations such as news companies, social media platforms, and educational institutions.

Advertising

Could use non-intrusive ads in a free version, limited to educational or research tools to maintain credibility.

Risk Analysis

Key Risks

Security and Privacy Risks

Identifies risks involving sensitive user input, improper storage or transmission of submitted content, and attempts to manipulate the AI model through adversarial inputs or repeated probing.

Reliability and Trust Risks

Highlights false positives, source bias, gaming of the system, and user overreliance on automated fact-checking as major concerns for the proposed system.
